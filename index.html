Albert Einstein

He was a genius who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. Einstein is best known by the general public for his mass–energy equivalence formula E = mc2 (which has been dubbed "the world's most famous equation").

Here is some facts about Einstein's life:

  • Born: 14 March 1879 in Ulm, Württemberg, Germany
  • Died: 18 April 1955 in Princeton, New Jersey, US
  • Family:
    • Einstein was the eldest child of Hermann Einstein, an electrical engineer and salesman, and Pauline Koch. He had one sister, named Maja.
    • Einstein married Mileva Marić in 1903.Together they had two sons.
    • After divorcing Einstein married his second wife, Elsa Löwenthal, in 1919.
  • His life:
    • In 1887 Einstein began his education at the Luitpold Gymnasium (a secondary school) in Munich.
    • In 1896, aged 17, Einstein enrolled at Zürich Polytechnic, where he studied for a mathematics and physics teaching diploma.
    • In 1905 he completed a doctorate degree from the University of Zurich.
    • After demonstrating his impressive work in theoretical physics, Einstein was made a privatdozent in Bern, Switzerland in 1908.
    • Einstein’s academic career developed further in 1914 when he became the director of the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute of Physics and a professor at the University of Berlin.
    • In 1915 Einstein published his work on the general theory of relativity, which suggests that “space and time are woven together, and become distorted by objects with mass”.
    • As a result of his outstanding work, in 1921 Einstein was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for services to theoretical physics.
    • In 1933 he renounced his German citizenship and emigrated to the US, where he became a professor at Princeton University in New Jersey.
    • In the 1940s Einstein also became involved in campaigns advocating civil rights in the US, and he joined National Association for the Advancement of Colored People
    • In the later years of his life, Einstein continued to work as a theoretical physicist, and he further developed his theory of general relativity.

In 1999, TIME Magazine named Einstein as the ‘Person of the Century’.
